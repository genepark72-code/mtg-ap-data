# Documentary Source Universe — Fantasy / Sci-Fi / Gaming Art

*The master checklist for evaluating an artist's `documentary_footprint`. Built for the "2050 art-historian comb": what survives is the institutional and documentary paper trail, not follower counts. Sources are tiered by durability/authority. An artist's footprint = the highest-tier sources they appear in, weighted by venue quality over count.*

---

## A. Institutional / of-record (highest durability)

**Museums & centers**
- **Norman Rockwell Museum** (Stockbridge) — *Enchanted: A History of Fantasy Illustration* (2020–23, traveled to Hunter Museum + Flint Institute of Arts); **Rockwell Center for American Visual Studies**; **illustrationhistory.org** (scholarly artist entries).
- **Society of Illustrators / Museum of American Illustration** (NY) — annuals, Hall of Fame, permanent collection, exhibitions.
- **Delaware Art Museum**, **Allentown Art Museum** (IlluxCon host), **Huntsville Museum of Art** (Donato solo).
- **The Frank Collection** (Jane & Howard Frank) — major documented private SF/F art collection.
- **National Portrait Gallery, London** (Baga — BP Portrait Award).

**Definitive history books / catalogs**
- **Enchanted: A History of Fantasy Illustration** — Jesse Kowalski (Abbeville, 2020). *Museum catalog; artist index is the authority for who was in the show.*
- **Dungeons & Dragons Art & Arcana: A Visual History** — Witwer/Newman/Peterson/Witwer (Ten Speed, 2018). *The definitive D&D art-history book.*
- **Spectrum: The Best in Contemporary Fantastic Art** — annual, juried, with written essays (Fenner → Flesk).
- **Infinite Worlds** (Di Fate); per-artist **monographs**; **Wikipedia** (biographical anchor).

## B. Documentary films (definitive, archival)

- **Eye of the Beholder: The Art of Dungeons & Dragons** (2019, Slagle/Stillman/Nacelle) — D&D-artist profiles. *Confirmed subjects incl. Brom, Erol Otus.*
- **Igniting the Spark – The Story of Magic: The Gathering** (2025, same team; Gen Con Best Documentary) — 70+ interviews. *Confirmed subjects incl. Donato, Rob Alexander, Winona Nelson, Jarel Threat, Mark Poole, Jeff Menges, Liz Danforth.*

## C. Independent documentary series & criticism

**Video**
- **Rhystic Studies** (Sam Gaglio, PhD) — long-form MTG art documentary essays + Substack profiles. *Dedicated "artist study" = a strong marker (e.g., Seb McKinnon).*
- **Spice8Rack** — MTG design/art/culture video essays.

**Written / blogs of record**
- **Muddy Colors** (founded by Dan Dos Santos) — the major imaginative-realism blog/community.
- **Gurney Journey** (James Gurney) — illustration criticism/education.
- **The Mirror Gallery / Vorthos Mike** (Mike Linnemann, Hipsters of the Coast) — sustained MTG art criticism + artist interviews.
- **Tor.com** art posts (Irene Gallo).

## D. Magazines of record

- **ImagineFX** — leading fantasy/digital-art magazine (Master Interviews, tutorials, features). *The workhorse periodical for this field.*
- **Communication Arts** (Illustration Annual); **Illustration Magazine** (The Book Palace, UK).
- **Juxtapoz / Hi-Fructose** — gallery-crossover coverage (Cai, Tran).

## E. Podcasts / oral history

- **The Illustrator's Studio** (NRM / Rockwell Center; Jesse Kowalski) — museum oral record. *Confirmed episodes: Donato, Eggleton, Alessandra Pisano (Ep 07), Charles Vess.*
- **Humans of Magic** (James Hsu); **MTG Artist Connection**; **Vorthos Cast**, **Goblin Lore**.
- **One Fantastic Week**, **Chris Oatley's ArtCast**, **Muddy Colors** podcast.

## F. Official publisher documentation (primary source, promo-tinged)

- **The Art of Magic: The Gathering** set-book series (VIZ/Wizards); **Secret Lair Artist Series**; magic.wizards.com artist features / "A Life of Planeswalking"; **Weekly MTG**.
- **D&D:** Dragon/Dungeon Magazine archives; sourcebook credits; **Worlds & Realms** (Adam Lee, 2024); 50th-anniversary content.

## G. Self / promo (lowest weight — discount)

- Artist's own site, YouTube, Kickstarter, Patreon, social media.

---

## Scoring guidance

- **A-tier footprint** = monograph and/or museum catalog/Wikipedia and/or a documentary-film profile and/or a dedicated Rhystic Studies study **+** institution-level presence (teaching, founded institution, Hall of Fame).
- **B-tier** = substantial coverage in ≥1 of-record source (NRM podcast, ImagineFX feature, Mirror Gallery feature, Art-of-MTG book, documentary-film appearance, own acclaimed art book).
- **C-tier** = minimal after a fair look across **all** the above — appears in databases/credits only. For a working MTG/D&D artist, treat C as "not yet a documentary *subject*," and verify against C-list sources before trusting it.
